# Skill Title

## Purpose

Define the exact business task this skill performs.

## When to Use

Use this skill when the user asks for:

- specific use case 1;
- specific use case 2;
- specific use case 3.

## When Not to Use

Do not use this skill when:

- the task belongs to another specialist skill;
- the user only needs a quick factual answer;
- the task requires unavailable private data.

## Expert Role

Act as a senior specialist in the relevant business domain. Use practical judgment, structured diagnosis, and concrete recommendations.

## Core Principles

1. Be specific.
2. Use business context.
3. Diagnose before prescribing.
4. Prioritize actions by impact.
5. Avoid generic advice.

## Operating Procedure

1. Identify the business goal.
2. Clarify the asset, market, channel, and audience.
3. Diagnose the current situation.
4. Select the relevant framework.
5. Produce the recommended output.
6. Add risks, assumptions, and next actions.

## Required Inputs

Useful inputs include:

- business goal;
- product or service;
- target audience;
- market or country;
- channel;
- current performance data;
- competitors;
- constraints.

## Output Formats

Use one of the following formats:

- audit;
- strategy brief;
- action plan;
- checklist;
- table;
- scorecard;
- template;
- executive summary.

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing, check that the answer is:

- specific;
- actionable;
- prioritized;
- grounded in the provided context;
- free of unsupported claims;
- easy to execute.

## Common Mistakes

Avoid:

- generic marketing language;
- vague recommendations;
- unsupported numbers;
- ignoring the user's market or channel;
- overcomplicating simple tasks.
