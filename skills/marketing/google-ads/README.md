# Google Ads Master Pack

This folder contains master skills for improving Google Ads business performance through reliable measurement, value-based optimization, acquisition-cost control, search-query quality, Performance Max inputs, budget strategy, and controlled experiments.

## Core Principle

Do not optimize ROAS, CPA, or CPC in isolation.

Use this decision order:

1. Validate conversions and conversion values.
2. Select the business objective.
3. Define guardrail metrics.
4. Diagnose the main bottleneck.
5. Apply the relevant specialist skill.
6. Change one major lever at a time.
7. Evaluate after conversion delay and sufficient data.

## Usage Logic

- Unsure what to optimize → `google-ads-performance-director`
- Tracking or values may be wrong → `conversion-value-tracking-auditor`
- Revenue or profit efficiency → `google-ads-roas-optimizer`
- Acquisition-cost efficiency → `google-ads-cpa-optimizer`
- Expensive clicks or weak relevance → `google-ads-cpc-quality-score-optimizer`
- Search terms, match types, or negatives → `search-campaign-query-optimizer`
- Performance Max → `performance-max-optimizer`
- Bidding strategy or budget allocation → `budget-bidding-strategy-planner`
- Controlled testing → `google-ads-experiment-planner`

## KPI Logic

### ROAS

Use when conversions have reliable and meaningfully different values. Include margin and conversion delay when setting targets.

### CPA

Use when conversion actions have similar business value or when qualified acquisition cost is the main constraint. Prefer quality-adjusted CPA when lead outcomes are available.

### CPC

Use as an efficiency and diagnostic metric, not the final business objective. Evaluate CPC together with CTR, conversion rate, CPA, ROAS, and traffic quality.
