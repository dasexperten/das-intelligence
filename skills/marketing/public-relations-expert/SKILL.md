# Public Relations Expert

## Purpose

Plan practical public relations work that builds credibility, visibility, trust, and authority for a brand, product, founder, launch, market entry, or business milestone.

This skill helps turn business news, expertise, product strengths, and proof points into media angles, outreach plans, press materials, and reputation-building actions.

## When to Use

Use this skill when the task is to:

- create a PR strategy;
- prepare a product or market launch announcement;
- write a media angle;
- plan founder or brand visibility;
- build trust with press, partners, retailers, or distributors;
- prepare press materials;
- organize outreach to journalists, bloggers, creators, or industry media.

## When NOT to Use

Do not use for crisis response, legal disputes, or reputation emergencies. Use Crisis Communications Expert for sensitive incidents.

## Inputs

Useful inputs:

- brand or company;
- product or announcement;
- target market;
- audience;
- media type;
- proof points;
- founder story;
- competitors;
- timing;
- desired reputation outcome.

## Workflow

1. Define the PR goal and audience.
2. Identify the newsworthy angle.
3. Separate brand story, product story, founder story, and market story.
4. Match angles to media types.
5. Build key messages and proof points.
6. Create press materials and outreach notes.
7. Prioritize media targets.
8. Define follow-up rhythm and success metrics.
9. Add risks and approval requirements.

## Output

Always provide:

- PR goal;
- target audience;
- media angle;
- key messages;
- proof points;
- press material checklist;
- media target categories;
- outreach plan;
- KPI table;
- risks and approvals;
- next steps.

## Best Practices

- Lead with a real story, not a brand slogan.
- Make the angle useful for the journalist or audience.
- Support claims with proof.
- Adapt the pitch to each media type.
- Keep outreach short and specific.
- Build relationships before needing coverage.

## Common Mistakes

- Sending generic press releases everywhere.
- Confusing advertising with PR.
- Making claims without evidence.
- Ignoring local market relevance.
- Pitching too early before the story is clear.
